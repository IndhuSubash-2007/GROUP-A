## Course Objectives

1. Understand the fundamental principles of electrical engineering.
2. Learn the working principles of fundamental electronic devices and circuits.
3. Gain an overview of basic concepts in different types of communication.

---

## Syllabus Modules

### Module 1 – DC Electric Circuits and Magnetic Circuits (11 Hours)

- Current and Voltage Division Rule, Relative potential  
- Capacitors & Inductors: V-I relations, Energy stored  
- Ohm's Law and Kirchhoff’s Laws  
- Star-delta conversion (resistive only)  
- DC Circuit Analysis: Mesh current method, matrix representation  
- Node voltage method  
- Elementary Magnetic Circuits: MMF, field strength, flux density, reluctance  
- Comparison between electric and magnetic circuits  
- Series/parallel magnetic circuits with composite materials  

### Module 2 – Electromagnetic Induction & AC Circuits (11 Hours)

- Faraday’s and Lenz's Laws  
- Self and mutual inductance, coefficient of coupling  
- AC Fundamentals: Sinusoidal waveforms, frequency, period, RMS, form factor  
- Phasor representations: Trigonometric, rectangular, polar, complex  
- AC Circuits: Resistive, inductive, capacitive; RL, RC, RLC circuits  
- Power factor, active, reactive, and apparent power  
- Three-phase systems: Generation, star/delta connections, voltage/current relations  

### Module 3 – Electronic Devices (13 Hours)

- Passive and active components  
- PN junction diode: Working, V-I characteristics  
- Zener diode, Zener regulator, breakdown mechanisms  
- Rectifiers: Half wave, full wave, bridge; ripple factor  
- BJT: Working, CE configuration, biasing, load line  
- Transistor as a switch/amplifier  
- RC coupled amplifier  
- Introduction to FET, MOSFETs: N- and P-channel  

### Module 4 – Modern Electronics & Communication (9 Hours)

- Communication system block diagram  
- Fiber optic system  
- AM and FM basics and receivers  
- Wired and wireless communication  
- GSM block diagram  
- 3G to 6G comparison  
- Electronic instrumentation: Multimeter, function generator, CRO, Lissajous patterns  
- IoT Applications: Smart homes, healthcare, agriculture (case studies)
